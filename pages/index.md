# Product Manager Internship

This is an Applied Economics & Product Manager Internship play book.

[Git Setup — how to submit feedback and update documents](git-setup)

# Agreement

Title: Applied Economics & Product Manager Intern
Duration: 8-10 weeks (flexible based on academic schedule)
Weekly Commitment: ~20-25 hours (adjusted as needed to support academic success)

## Objective

Develop practical, real-world understanding of economic principles by working in a structured, corporate-style environment to build and validate a small product with potential economic value.

## Core Responsibilities

1. Product Develoment & Execution

- Design and build a small, focused product or tool
- Deliver a working prototype within the first 3 to 5 weeks
- Iterate based on user feedback

2. Applied Economics (Research & Testing)

- Conduct user interviews and research
- Develop and test assumptions about demand, pricing and user behavior
- Apply concepts from microeconmics coursework to real decisions

3. Professional Communication & Operations

- Participate in weekly planning and review meetings
- Submit structued weekly written updates
- Present progress, challenges and next steps

4. Documentation & Portfolio Development

- Maintain clear documentation of work
- Product visible artifacts (code, reports, demos)
- Complete a final case study summarizing outcomes and learnings

## Work Structure

- Monday - weekly planning
- Mideweek - async update
- Friday - Showcase, review and retrospective + feedback
- Daily - Daily synch as needed

## Output

- 2 to 4 portfolio-ready artifacts
    - GitHub repo of an app/program
    - Product landing page
    - Running, visible product or prototype (URL)
    - One pager describing problem/pain point and solution theory

## Strech Goal (Market Validation)

Hitting out of the park if you can provide evidence of user demand (usage, feedback, or revenue).

The goal is not just to build something – but to understand how and why value is created, tested, and communicated in the real world.

# First 2 Weeks

## Week 1: Orientation + Direction

**Day 1 (kickoff)**

Walk through this internship doc together and ellaborate on expectations, weekly rythm and what *good* looks like.

**Idea** We're going to build something small that people might pay for.

Take a moment to write down what do you want to get out of this and what you think building something *valuable* means.

**Day 2**

Brainstorm 5 - 8 product ideas and discuss each briefly.

Pick top 3 ideas and for each, write down:

- Who is it for?
- What problem does it solve?
- Why would someone care?

**Day 3**

Let's review the 3 ideas together and push hard on who actually has this problem and how do they solve it today.

From this, choose 1 idea (could be something totally different than from the 3) and write a one-pager – a 1-page Product Brief that states the problem, who has it, and theory on how to solve it.

**Day 4**

Discuss the pitfalls of user interviews and what to avoid.

Identify 5 people to talk to and schedule or initiate outreach.

**Day 5**

Ideally, you conduct 2 to 3 conversations.

### Deliverables

- Notes from conversations - insights
- Update your understanding of the problem

## Week 2: Real-World Input + First Decisions

**Day 6**

Monday planning session. Review what you learned from the previous week.

- What surprised you?
- Did anyone actually care?

It's likely that you'll have to refine the problem hypothesis.

**Day 7**

Define a target user – Ideal Customer Profile – and pick the core feature.

Your statement should be of the form, "This product helps _____ do _____ by _____".

**Day 8**

Let's introduce the concept of pricing. 

Pick a price range and ask 2 to 3 users, "Would you pay for this?". We're looking for a signal.

**Day 9**

What is the absolute simplest version we can build? This is sometimes referred to as the Minimum Viable Product or Minimum Viable Problem.

Break it into tasks and prepare for Monday's planning session.

**Day 10**

Present the problem (hypothesis), the user, the proposed solution and what you learned from users.

Expect direct and honest feedback, go/no-go on idea or refinement.
